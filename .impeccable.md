# vellum — design context

## Audience
A single user — someone keeping a long-form personal notebook / journal / blog. Sole author, occasional readers. Emphasis on reading and writing comfort, not engagement metrics.

## Use cases
Editorial-style personal site / notebook / archive of essays, daily notes, reading log. Long-form text is the dominant content type; photography appears as the secondary anchor.

## Tone & personality
- Editorial / paper-bound: feels like a hand-bound journal, not a SaaS product.
- Warm, slow, intentional. Cream paper, ink-brown text, terracotta accent — never neon.
- Refined minimalism with texture. Hairlines, gentle shadows, paper grain — not flat / not glassy.
- Confident typographic hierarchy: large display serif headings against quiet sans body.

## Stack
- Tailwind v4, CSS-first `@theme` (all tokens in `src/input.css`).
- Plain HTML during prototyping; will port to **Astro** later. Components should be authored so the panels in `index.html` map 1:1 to `.astro` files (`<Hero/>`, `<ColorTheory/>`, `<Typography/>`, etc.).
- Google Fonts: **Crimson Pro**, **Inter**, **IBM Plex Mono**. Do not substitute.

## Palette (extracted from reference, not invented)
- Paper background: `#F6F2E9` · surface: `#F7F3EB` · raised: `#FBF8F1`
- Ink text: `#1F1C18` (darkest) → `#3C4041` (softer) → `#605E5B` (muted)
- Hairline: `#C6C4BD` · borders soft: `#E2D9C6`
- Clay accent (terracotta): `#B45828` primary, `#DF8D64` light, `#573317` deep
- Leather / photographic browns: `#51331B` → `#AF876C`

## Anti-goals
- No glassmorphism, no neon gradients, no "AI cyan-on-dark".
- No icon-with-rounded-square-bg above every heading.
- No identical card grids — vary panel sizes (this is what the reference does).
- No bouncy easing. Motion stays calm.
- No dark theme yet — confirm light first.

## Differentiation
The "one thing someone remembers": a personal notebook that looks like a *book*, not a product. The Crimson Pro display headings + IBM Plex Mono labels + warm-paper palette is the signature.
